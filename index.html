Why does this even exist?

So here's the thing — my render was taking way longer than expected, and touching anything on this computer while Maya is running? Absolutely terrifying. One wrong click and goodbye, 3 hours of work.

So instead of staring at the render progress bar, I used the waiting time to build this little page before graduation. My major is Computer Science, and I wanted to do something meaningful with code before finishing school.

Process Log

A breakdown of what was built, how it was constructed, and why every decision supports the ANCHORED atmosphere.

01

What is Shown

This animation shows a small cozy house environment based on the atmosphere category "THE ANCHORED — Comfort." The scene begins with a wide exterior shot of the house and slowly moves closer before entering the interior space.

Inside the house, the camera focuses on furniture, lighting, and small decorative objects to create a calm and welcoming feeling.

The project focuses more on atmosphere and emotional comfort than narrative.

02

How it is Built

The environment was created in Autodesk Maya using a low poly art style. Most objects were modeled manually using simple polygon techniques. Arnold Renderer was used for lighting and rendering.

Warm lighting, pastel colors, and soft shadows were used throughout to create a cozy atmosphere. Additional area lights were placed near the windows to simulate sunlight entering the room.

Some detailed decorative objects were created in Nomad Sculpt on iPad and imported into Maya. The camera animation was created using keyframes — intentionally slow and smooth.

03

Why it Supports the Atmosphere

This project supports the "THE ANCHORED — Comfort" category through warm lighting, enclosed spaces, soft colors, and slow camera movement. The small house and interior decorations were designed to feel safe, peaceful, and personal.

Instead of using dramatic movement or intense lighting, the project focuses on calm visuals and comfortable pacing to create a relaxing atmosphere.
